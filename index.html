HIV/AIDS Status in the Philippines End of Year 2024

As of December 2024, the Philippines is grappling with a rapidly escalating HIV/AIDS crisis. The country is projected to have approximately 215,400 people living with HIV (PLHIV) by year-end, with an average of about 50 new diagnoses daily. Of these cases, nearly all are among males (95%), and a significant proportion occur among individuals aged 15-24 years. Despite these challenges, only about 64% of PLHIV are on antiretroviral therapy (ART). If interventions do not improve significantly, the number of PLHIV could reach approximately 448,000 by 2030.

Interconnected Challenges in the Philippines' HIV/AIDS Crisis

The Philippines continues to face significant challenges in its HIV/AIDS crisis. The country is experiencing one of the fastest-growing epidemics in Asia, with an average of about 55 new diagnoses daily10. By March 2024, there were 129,772 cumulative diagnosed cases, and only about 64% of people living with HIV are on antiretroviral therapy (ART)910. Late detection remains a major issue, with a notable increase in advanced HIV disease cases posing severe health risks11. Additionally, nearly half of new infections occur among individuals aged 15-24 years, predominantly affecting men who have sex with men (MSM)9. These interconnected challenges highlight the need for improved early testing and treatment adherence to combat the epidemic effectively.
